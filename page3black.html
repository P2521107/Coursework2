The Elizabethan Era

Through history, the desired look was always to appear pale skinned. The Queen of England Elizabeth I, during her reign between 1558 to when she died in 1603, was the leading model of this fashion trend. Women would create ceruse, which is a mixture of lead and vinegar to apply to the face and neck to create a pale skinned look. When looking at images of Elizabeth I, it is noticeable that her hairline is pulled far back, exposing a large forehead. This most likely occurred due to the lead in the ceruse mixture causing hair loss. During this period, many women died due to lead poisoning, or in extreme cases they would have muscle paralysis which then led to death. Queen Elizabeth I however did not die of lead poisoning, she fell ill and also became severely depressed due to the loss of a few friends during that time.
